TRIANGULAR ARBITRAGE IN NIGERIAN BDC MARKET¶

THE CASE OF GBP-USD-NGN¶

This notebook investigates the availability of triangular arbitrgae opportunities in Nigerian Bureau de Chnage markets. It specifically focus on the case of GBP-USD-NGN. In this case, a triangular arbitrage opportunity exists when the amount of Naira gotten from the indirect (3-way, 2-step) exchange of GBP to USD to NGN exceeds the direct (2-way, 1-step).

JOHN'S DILEMMA¶

For practical purposes, a hypothetical real life example illustrates the case: John has a special passport that allows him to bypass travel restrictions and thus, travel from UK to Nigeria during the COVID-19 pandemic. He has already paid for his flight so we will ignore those costs. John has GBP notes and wants to change it to NGN. He has two choices:

  1. Change it to Naira directly
  2. Change it to Dollar in then change the Dollar to Naira i.e indirect change

If a triangular arbitrage opportunity does exist then John gets more money in the second scenario as opposed to the first. It is typically very difficult to find these opportunities and even more difficult to execute.

WHY DOES THIS EXIST OR NOT EXIST?¶

CAUSES AND FRICTIONS¶

It is diffucult to find because BDCs will buy a currency from you at a rate lower than which they will sell. This is how they make their profit. Let's call this a 'market friction'. Thus, executing a 2-step (3-way, indirect) exchange whould cost more as there are two levels (as opposed to one level) of this market friciton. But in times like this (i.e COVID-19 pandemic), the rush towards the Dollar (safe haven currency) as opposed to the Pound coupled with restrictions on exchanging currency at banks can create some discrepancies between the global GBP/USD exchange rate and the implied local GBP/USD exchange rate which allows some profit to be made in the process.

EXECUTION DIFFICULTIES¶

It is difficult to execute because there is some logistics involved i.e no one (that I am aware of) has this 'special' passport. There are several alternatives to travelling if John is already in Nigeria. He just needs to find a way of changing his GBP to USD at the official global rate (ordinarily, if he does so in Nigeria he will do so at the implied BDC rate which will neutralise the profit potential). John could:

  1. Have a 'special' bank account that allows him to deposit any currency and withdraw internationally in the currency of his choice (GBP or USD) i.e. deposit his GBP --> withdraw in USD (will be done at official global rate) --> chnage to Naira at BDC
  2. Could meet someone in Nigeria with USD willing and to do the GBP to USD exchange at the global official rate i.e. exchange GBP for USD at global official rate --> change USD to GBP at local BDC rate
  3. Some other means of converting GBP to USD at the global officialexchange rate

JUST WANT TO MAKE A PROFIT?¶

What if John has no use for Naira now and just wants to make a profit in GBP from this opportunity? John could add an extra step of changing his NGN to GBP and realising a profit (if one exists). This can be donw by depotiting the NGN in the special bank account in (1) above The steps will now be:

  1. John has GBP
  2. John changes GBP to USD in UK
  3. John changes USD to NGN in a Nigerian BDC
  4. John changes NGN to GBP in a Nigerian BDC
  5. ASSUMING HE HAS THE 'SPECiAL' bank account: deposit the NGN in the account and withdraw in GBP
  6. At this point he should have more GBP than he started with if the triangular arbitrage existed

4) Calculate potential profits from the triangular arbitrage¶

In this section, I calculate the percentage profit (returns) from the triangular arbitrade if executed on each of the last 15 days at the rates corresponding to those days. Note: If an efficient means of making the exchanges at both global rates (for GBP to USD) and local rates (for USD to NGN and NGN to USD), the executor could possibly execute this trade several times in a single day and thus, increase the daily profit potential.

CONCLUSION¶

The graph above shows that for the last four days, an arbitrage opportunity has existed. This can be exploited by anyone who is able to bypass the logistical constraint (e.g. has the 'special' bank account) and has enough money to dedicate to the trade. As mentioned earlier, he/she could possibly execute this trade many times in a day if the logistics are efficient. If executed 8 timess in a day (once every working hour), the average 4% will become (($1.04^8$ - 1) * 100 = 36% returns in a single day. How's that for alchemy.

It also shows that this opportunity did not exist in the 11 days prior. In fact there is a loss associated with the trade. This is due to the'market friction' referenced in the introduction (i.e BDC operators sell currency at a higher price than they buy it in order to make a profit).

Attempting to forecast how long such an opportunity will remain is most likely an exercise in vain. It will depend on largely unpredictable global macroeconomic factors (due to the COVID-19 pandemic's effect on global markets and CBN policy on buying currency) and also on local microeconimic factors (Other Arbitrageurs may wipe away this opportunity by taking advantage of it i.e. supply and demand will reach equilibrium). One could also utilise data from the last time such an opportunity arose (e.g 2015 currency devaluation and consequent CBN restrictions) to detect patterns that may be indicative of the timeline of this opportunity. But it is likely that the sample set will be too specific to that time to draw meaningful conclusions. Could be fun to try anyways. An attempt to forecast it may be done in another notebook but don't hold your breath.

While arbitrage by definition is riskless, it should be noted that there are limitations for the results of this notebook:

  1. Exchange actually change in real-time. In this notebook, I have used a singular (opening price) rate for each day.
  2. In this notebook, while buying and selling price discrpancy is accounted for in the BDC rates (GBP to USD and USD to NGN) it is ignored in the GBP/USD rate (the discrepancy is usually much smaller)
  3. Speed of execution is a factor in arbitrage as rates change in real-time
  4. I have relied on the data in AbokiFX.com which may or may not be trade-able depending on if you can find the BDC operator who provides the quotes (Should be very possible)
  5. If you can think of other reasons I might be missing out (most likely exist), please contact me on LinkedIn: Olaoluwapo Oyefolu, or comment (if on blog).
